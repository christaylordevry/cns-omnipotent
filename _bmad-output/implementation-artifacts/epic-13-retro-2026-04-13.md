No WriteGate changes, no new Vault IO tools.

## Systems lessons

- **Mobile is a trust-labeling problem first:** operators sync the same bytes they govern on desktop; the win is **naming** which surfaces produce **audit-grade** mutations versus **disk edits** that PAKE and mutation logs never see.  
- **Dual AGENTS mirrors are a deliberate tax:** vault and spec copies stayed aligned through review (see-also paths, applied-vs-paste snippet wording). Future edits should touch **both** or record an explicit exception.  
- **Nexus coexistence copy matters:** “read-only aware,” **triage back to Inbox**, and **Nexus not on phone** are short phrases that prevent an entire class of wrong mobile architectures.

## Continuity from Epic 12

Brain charter stresses **out-of-band indexing** and protected paths. Mobile doc stresses **out-of-band disk edits** on synced content. Together they tell a coherent story: **not everything touching the vault is Vault IO**.

## Continuity from Epic 14

Routing readout classifies mobile as **soft** dependency for **device class** (input constraints, offline). If a future mobile client ever becomes a **first-class execution surface**, that classification must be revisited—not assumed stable forever.
